# Beta Usability Research Plan

This document is a plan for five moderated sessions. No sessions have been conducted and no results are claimed.

## Study goal

Learn whether a new listener can reach first value in the solo experience and collaborate in a Vibe Room without coaching. Identify vocabulary, wayfinding, recovery, trust, and accessibility breakdowns before choosing the next headline feature.

## Participants and logistics

- Five music listeners with a mix of streaming habits and technical confidence; include mobile-first participants and recruit disabled participants/access-technology users through appropriate, compensated channels where possible.
- 45–60 minutes each, remote or in person, using the participant's preferred device when feasible.
- Moderator, optional silent note-taker, beta environment, and two test accounts for the collaboration task.
- Obtain informed consent before beginning. Recording is **off by default**; ask separately for recording permission and continue with notes only if declined. Avoid collecting real memories or sensitive prompts—invite fictional/test content.
- Remind participants this tests the product, not them; they can pause, skip a task, or withdraw at any time.

## Moderator opening

1. Explain the purpose, session length, data handling, and no-recording default.
2. Confirm consent and accessibility accommodations.
3. Ask the participant to think aloud, while avoiding hints about labels or locations.
4. Warm-up: “How do you usually discover music, and what makes a song feel personally meaningful?”

## Task script

Give one prompt at a time. Do not name the control or route that completes it.

1. **Orient and join** — “You just heard about VibeVerse. Show me what you think it does, then create an account.”
2. **First saved track** — “Find a song you like and keep it in your collection.”
3. **Memory** — “Attach a short fictional memory and a mood to that song, then find it again in your journal.”
4. **AI playlist** — “Create a playlist for a specific feeling or situation. Tell me what you expect before you submit, then assess the result.” Use a study key/budget or a preconfigured stub; never use a participant's paid account.
5. **Taste and Galaxy** — “Find what VibeVerse says about your taste, then use the visual map to learn something about one artist.” Seed enough test data beforehand so eligibility is not the task.
6. **Create a room** — “Start a listening room you could invite a friend to. How would you share access?”
7. **Collaborate** — A facilitator joins from the second account. “Confirm they arrived, react to the room, and decide what should play next.” Queue content can be pre-seeded to avoid testing provider availability.
8. **Recover and exit** — “Leave the room, then return using the information you would have shared. Finally, show how you would sign out.”

After each task ask: “What did you expect?”, “What, if anything, made you hesitate?”, and “How confident are you that it worked?” End with: “Which part felt most valuable?”, “Which part would you ignore?”, and “What would you be uncomfortable storing here?”

## Rubric

Rate each task from observed behavior, not moderator interpretation:

- **Pass:** completes without a hint; progress and success are understood.
- **Hesitation:** completes independently but pauses, backtracks, misinterprets feedback, or makes one recoverable error.
- **Failure:** cannot complete, abandons, requires a directional hint, or reaches the wrong outcome without recognizing it.

Also record time on task, number/type of errors, recoveries, confidence (1–5), and a verbatim post-task ease comment. A moderator clarification that merely repeats the prompt is not a hint; naming a destination/control is.

## Synthesis template

1. Build an affinity map from observations, keeping contradictory behavior visible.
2. For every theme, list participant count, affected tasks, evidence snippets, and counterexamples.
3. Prioritize by task blockage/user harm, frequency, and confidence: P0 blocker/harm, P1 major friction, P2 recoverable issue, P3 polish.
4. Separate observed findings from hypotheses and facilitator/provider failures.
5. Produce a task matrix across all five sessions and identify the exact step where each failure arc broke.
6. Recommend bounded changes and the evidence needed to validate them; do not infer demand from polite praise.

Success for the study is credible evidence, not a predetermined score. The provisional beta target—four of five participants completing the core discovery journey without assistance—should be evaluated only after the sessions occur.
