# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Music enthusiasts who want a more personal, emotional, and visual relationship with their listening history. They use VibeVerse to discover real music, preserve the memories attached to songs, and understand their taste without needing technical knowledge of AI or music metadata.

## Product Purpose

VibeVerse turns a listener's saved music and memories into an explorable personal universe. It combines catalog search, a music journal, AI-guided playlist generation, taste profiling, and a visual music map. Success means discovery feels personal and surprising while every generated recommendation remains understandable, playable, and grounded in real recordings.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, intimate, and assured. The product speaks warmly and concisely, with a light cosmic vocabulary that supports comprehension rather than becoming decoration. It should feel like a deep-space music observatory and a trusted DJ, not a generic productivity tool.

## Anti-references

- A Spotify clone or conventional streaming-library interface.
- A generic SaaS dashboard made from interchangeable cards and metrics.
- Constant neon, gradients, or motion that compete with the music.
- AI interfaces that expose implementation jargon or make unexplained recommendations.
- Dark patterns, autoplay, inaccessible contrast, or interactions that depend on hover.

## Design Principles

1. Make the listener the center of the universe: personal context and memories should shape discovery without trapping it.
2. Earn the theatrical moment: each page gets one distinctive interaction; the surrounding interface stays quiet and useful.
3. Explain the recommendation: AI output should remain legible, specific, and connected to real music.
4. Motion should imply sound: transitions pulse, drift, and ease without delaying the task.
5. Design for a credible demo and a real product: every state, control, and result must work with live data and graceful failure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve visible keyboard focus, semantic labels, 44px touch targets, text contrast of at least 4.5:1, and complete reduced-motion alternatives. Never rely on color, gradient, hover, or motion as the only carrier of meaning. Audio previews require explicit user action and only one may play at a time.
